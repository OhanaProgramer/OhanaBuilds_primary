What we do

We build practical websites for nonprofits and community groups that need clear communication without technical overhead.

Our approach uses proven website foundations, accessible structure, and straightforward support so staff and volunteers can keep content current with less effort.

Who benefits

  • Nonprofits and grassroots groups with limited funding or staff
  • New or early-stage organizations that need a clear, credible web presence
  • Community programs that need service information easy to find
  • Teams that value calm, trustworthy design without ongoing technical overhead

How It Works

A simple three-step process to launch quickly with clear expectations.

  1. Define your goals

    Share the services, audience needs, and core pages you need first.

  2. Share your content

How this work is supported

Ohana Builds is sustained through a mix of small project fees, general support, and occasional donations. We keep costs low for community organizations, and when funding is a barrier we can sometimes help cover essentials like a domain name or basic hosting.

The only direct, unavoidable cost for most sites is the domain name. Beyond that, support is flexible and voluntary—often as little as the equivalent of $1 per month, or whatever your organization feels is reasonable in your country or context. There are no hidden costs. Any expenses (such as domain or hosting) are discussed and agreed on before anything goes live.
